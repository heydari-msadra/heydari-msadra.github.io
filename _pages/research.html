Working Papers

R&D Networks with Heterogeneous Firms [Draft PDF] [Code]

with Zafer Kanik and Santiago Montoya-Blandón .

This paper develops a model of endogenous R&D network formation among firms with heterogeneous abilities to convert R&D into cost reductions. By distinguishing high- and low-productivity firms, the model predicts a wider range of stable network structures than the fully connected case typical in the literature. In a four-firm benchmark, we show that when productivity gaps are large, the Positive Assortative (PA) network, where similar firms collaborate, is both pairwise stable and potentially welfare-improving. Simulation results confirm these findings for larger and more diverse settings, highlighting that targeted support for high-productivity firms can shift the economy from a fully connected to a welfare-enhancing assortative network.

Absolute Intragenerational Income Mobility in Iran (2023) [Journal Link] [PDF/Working Paper] [Code]

with Naser Amanzadeh . Published in The Quarterly Review of Economics and Finance. 90: 38-50.

This study utilized Absolute Intra-generational Income Mobility (AIIM) as an additional measure to traditional income distribution indices, providing a clearer view of economic conditions by highlighting household income dynamics. We estimated AIIM for urban households in Iran from 2011 to 2019 using cross-sectional data, and extended these estimates backward to 1991 by leveraging copula distributions and models.
