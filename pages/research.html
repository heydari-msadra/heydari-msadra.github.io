R&D Networks under Heterogeneous Firm Productivities [arXiv] (Supplementary Appendix) [Slides] {code}

with Zafer Kanik and Santiago Montoya-Blandón.

We study R&D network formation with heterogeneous productivities, generalizing the homogeneous framework in Goyal & Moraga-Gonzalez (2001). Heterogeneity creates asymmetric gains for connecting firms: the less productive firm benefits disproportionately, while the more productive firm exerts greater R&D effort and costs. For sufficiently large productivity gaps between firms, benchmark results on pairwise stable networks are overturned: the complete network becomes unstable, whereas the Positive Assortative (PA) network—where firms cluster by productivity levels—emerges as stable. Simulations show the PA structure delivers higher welfare compared to the complete network. Altogether, a counterintuitive finding emerges: higher average R&D productivity in the economy may reduce welfare through (i) changing the stable structure of R&D networks and/or (ii) causing a crowding-out effect of high-productive firms.

Absolute Intragenerational Income Mobility in Iran (2023) [Journal Link] [PDF] [Video-Fa]

with Naser Amanzadeh. Published in The Quarterly Review of Economics and Finance (2023), 90: 38–50.

This study uses Absolute Intragenerational Income Mobility (AIIM) to complement standard inequality measures by focusing on income transitions rather than static distributions. Using cross-sectional Iranian household data (2011–2019), AIIM is estimated and extended back to 1991 using copula-based modeling, revealing long-term mobility patterns obscured in traditional inequality indices.
