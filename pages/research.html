We study R&D network formation with heterogeneous productivities, generalizing the homogeneous framework in Goyal & Moraga-Gonzalez (2001). Heterogeneity creates asymmetric gains for connecting firms: the less productive firm benefits disproportionately, while the more productive firm exerts greater R&D effort and costs. Simulation evidence shows that PA networks deliver higher welfare compared to complete networks.
